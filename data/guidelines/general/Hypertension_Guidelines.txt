
CLINICAL GUIDELINE: HYPERTENSION MANAGEMENT

Based on: AHA/ACC Hypertension Guidelines

CLASSIFICATION:
- Normal: <120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120-129/<80 mmHg
- Stage 1: 130-139/80-89 mmHg
- Stage 2: ≥140/90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: ≥180/120 mmHg

BLOOD PRESSURE TARGETS:
- General population: <130/80 mmHg
- Elderly (≥65 years): <130/80 mmHg
- Diabetes: <130/80 mmHg
- CKD: <130/80 mmHg

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS (All Patients):
1. Weight loss (if overweight)
   - Goal: BMI <25
   - Each 1 kg loss → 1 mmHg reduction

2. DASH Diet
   - Rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains
   - Low in saturated fat
   - Sodium <2300mg/day (ideally <1500mg/day)

3. Physical Activity
   - 150 minutes/week moderate aerobic exercise
   - Or 75 minutes/week vigorous exercise

4. Limit Alcohol
   - Men: ≤2 drinks/day
   - Women: ≤1 drink/day

5. Smoking Cessation

PHARMACOLOGIC TREATMENT:

Stage 1 Hypertension (130-139/80-89):
- If ASCVD risk ≥10%: Start medication + lifestyle
- If ASCVD risk <10%: Lifestyle modification, reassess in 3-6 months

Stage 2 Hypertension (≥140/90):
- Start medication immediately + lifestyle modifications
- Consider 2-drug combination if BP ≥20/10 above goal

FIRST-LINE MEDICATIONS:

1. Thiazide Diuretics
   - Chlorthalidone 12.5-25mg daily
   - Hydrochlorothiazide 12.5-50mg daily

2. ACE Inhibitors
   - Lisinopril 10-40mg daily
   - Enalapril 5-40mg daily
   - Ramipril 2.5-20mg daily

3. ARBs (if ACE-intolerant)
   - Losartan 25-100mg daily
   - Valsartan 80-320mg daily

4. Calcium Channel Blockers
   - Amlodipine 2.5-10mg daily
   - Diltiazem ER 120-540mg daily

COMBINATION THERAPY:
Preferred combinations:
- ACE inhibitor/ARB + CCB
- ACE inhibitor/ARB + Thiazide
- CCB + Thiazide

Avoid: ACE inhibitor + ARB (increased risk)

SPECIAL POPULATIONS:

African American Patients:
- CCB or thiazide diuretic preferred first-line
- May require higher doses or more medications

Pregnancy:
- Labetalol, methyldopa, or nifedipine
- AVOID: ACE inhibitors, ARBs

CKD:
- ACE inhibitor or ARB preferred (proteinuria benefit)
- Monitor potassium and creatinine

Elderly (≥65 years):
- "Start low, go slow"
- Same targets but gradual titration
- Monitor for orthostatic hypotension

RESISTANT HYPERTENSION:
Definition: Uncontrolled BP despite ≥3 medications (including diuretic)

Evaluation:
- Confirm true resistance (home BP monitoring)
- Rule out secondary causes
- Check medication adherence
- Review for interfering substances

Management:
- Optimize diuretic therapy (chlorthalidone preferred)
- Add spironolactone 25-50mg daily
- Consider referral to hypertension specialist
